# Employee Attrition Analysis Project

## 1. Why this analysis is important
Employee attrition causes losses for organizations from various aspects:
* **Financial Costs:** Including recruitment costs, severance costs, and training and rehabilitation costs.
* **Loss of Competencies:** The loss of employees who possess knowledge and experience within the organization.
* **Impact on Remaining Staff:** The workload of the vacant position is carried by the remaining staff until a replacement is found.
* **The "Contagion Effect":** Other employees may believe the company is unstable or that there are better opportunities outside the company.

## 2. Insights and Solutions
one of the insights and findings I discussed in the pdf was:
### Findings
   * **83%** of employees who left had a **performance rating of 3**. This is a danger zone because we are losing productive employees.
   * This percentage is divided into **55%** of employees belonging to the **lowest salary category** and **28%** otherwise.
    * The **55%** is further divided into:
        * **25%** with more than **two years of experience** in the organization.
        * **30%** who are **new hires** (0 to 2 years).
    * The **30%** is divided into:
        * **12%** who were trained **1 to 2 times**.
        * **18%** who were trained **more than 3 times**.

### Insights
* The organization is losing high-performing employees.
* Sufficient salaries are not being given to employees with average or high performance.
* Experienced employees (over 2 years) are in the lowest salary category.
* New employees (30%) who were trained more than 3 times have the highest attrition rate.
* **Conclusion:** We can assume there is a problem with the training provided to employees, which makes them leave, or that because their performance is high—along with the training they receive—they feel the salary is insufficient. In this case, **"I am training my employees for the competitor."**

---

## 3. Recommendations
* **Salary Policy Review:** Review salary determination policies for the high-performing employee category, especially those with more than two years of experience.
* **Training Audit:** Examine the quality and type of training provided to new hires (1 to 2 years) and gather their opinions on the extent of benefit from the training.
* **Incentives:** Provide incentives and rewards for completing training.
